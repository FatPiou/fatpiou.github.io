Scoring:

7 How scoring works: the first "point" is 15. The scoring goes: love (0), 15, 30, 40, then game.
If the score is 40-40, then it's called a deuce. You have to win two times in a row,
so if you win the point, and you're serving, it's called "advantage server". If you're receiving,
then, clearly it's "advantage receiver". The players can also opt to do no-advantage, which means the first one to win the next point wins. The first player to win 6 games wins the set. However, you need to win by 2.
So for example, if the score is 6-4, then the person with 6 wins.
Tiebreakers:
If the score is 6-5, then a tiebreaker starts.
you start from zero, and using normal numbering, go up until one hits 7 points.
If it's 6-5, but the opponent wins a point, becoming 6-6, then keep going until someone wins by 2.
Serves in Tiebreakers: The player who would normally be serving after 6â€“6 is the one to serve first in the tiebreak, and the tiebreak is considered a service game for this player.
The server begins his or her service from the deuce court and serves one point.
After the first point, the serve changes to the first server's opponent.
Each player then serves two consecutive points for the remainder of the tiebreak.
The first of each two-point sequence starts from the server's advantage court and the second starts from the deuce court.
In this way, the sum of the scores is even when the server serves from the deuce court.
After every six points, the players switch ends of the court;
note that the side-changes during the tiebreak will occur in the middle of a server's two-point sequence.
At the end of the tiebreak, the players switch ends of the court again, since the set score is always odd (13 games).

Court

The base line, at the very end of each side, is wehre the players start from. Those long rectangular"boxes"
on each side are called the alleys. The outer line is the doubles sideline, while the inner one is the singles sideline.
The vertical line nearest to the center is called the service line. The horizontal line in the middle is called the center service line.
The shorter boxes in the middle are called the service boxes. From the players POV, the deuce side is their right side.
You start on the deuce side, and serve on to the opposite service box.
You play one game, then switch sides. then play two games, and switch again.
